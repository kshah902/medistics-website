What is Advanced Primary Care Management?

Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) is CMS's newest care management program, designed to modernize and simplify how primary care practices deliver chronic care services. Unlike traditional Chronic Care Management (CCM), APCM eliminates the complex time-tracking requirements that have long been a barrier to program adoption.

APCM provides a unified approach to longitudinal patient management, consolidating multiple care management services into a single, streamlined program. This means practices can focus on delivering quality care rather than documenting minutes.

Why APCM matters: For many practices, APCM represents the future of care management‚Äîsimpler to implement, easier to sustain, and better aligned with how modern primary care actually works.

Key Benefits of APCM

  • No time tracking required ‚Äî Eliminates the administrative burden of documenting 20+ minutes of care management activities
  • Tiered reimbursement ‚Äî Payment levels based on patient complexity, ensuring appropriate compensation for high-acuity patients
  • Simplified monthly billing ‚Äî Straightforward billing structure without complex time-based code selection
  • Works with add-on services ‚Äî Combine APCM with RPM, RTM, and BHI for comprehensive patient care
  • Reduced audit risk ‚Äî Without time tracking requirements, documentation is simpler and audit exposure decreases
  • Better patient outcomes ‚Äî More focus on care delivery, less on administrative documentation

Who Qualifies for APCM?

APCM is designed for Medicare beneficiaries who require longitudinal care management. Eligible patients typically include those with:

  • Multiple chronic conditions requiring ongoing management
  • Complex care needs that benefit from regular monitoring and coordination
  • Conditions that would traditionally qualify for CCM services
  • Need for care coordination across multiple providers or specialists
